Sharks

Sharks are an important part of our oceans natural ecosystem, managing various species of life within our oceans by keeping their prey in check so that one level of that ecosystem doesn't overpower and destroy another. Our oceans are in danger by a multitude of actions humans have taken: Dumping non-degradible trash and/or recycable material which lingers in or on our oceans; increased production of carbon emmitions that have caused rabid global warming, which has been melting our planets glacial resevoirs and the mass bleaching of reef ecosystems; Over fishing that has caused some species of fish and preditors that eat them to become endangered or extinct; as well as the overhunting of sharks that have led to a good number of sharks to become extinct or endangered, many of which are now recovering due to reducing and restricting hunting of sharks.
